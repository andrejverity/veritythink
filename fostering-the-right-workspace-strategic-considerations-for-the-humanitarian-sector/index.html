Fostering the Right Workspace - Strategic Considerations for the Humanitarian Sector

Fostering the Right Workspace - Strategic Considerations for the Humanitarian Sector

Written with Emma Amaral; October 2018

Read the full report

AI generated summary:

Optimizing workspace strategies is essential for humanitarian organizations to enhance their operational effectiveness and successfully navigate complex crisis environments. This report examines the strategic balance between physical, social, and digital environments, highlighting how each workspace type uniquely facilitates collaboration, innovation, and data sharing throughout the lifecycle of both sudden-onset and protracted emergencies. By strategically designing these spaces, organizations can better foster social capital, maintain a vital presence on the ground, and leverage the responsiveness of modern communication tools.

The research underscores that physical and social spaces are critical for building "swift trust"—a necessity in the chaotic, high-stakes initial phases of disaster response where temporary groups must coordinate quickly. In-person interactions are essential not only for establishing relationships among responders but also for fostering meaningful engagement with local governments and affected populations. Furthermore, physical workspaces serve as hubs for iterative problem-solving and innovation, offering a neutral ground where diverse stakeholders can align on narratives and improve the quality of data exchange.

While digital tools have revolutionized the humanitarian sector by lowering transaction costs and enabling global connectivity, they are most effective when paired with established in-person rapport. The findings suggest that technology amplifies existing levels of trust; therefore, virtual collaboration is best suited for routine, standardized tasks, or when supported by strong personal connections to the field. To maximize productivity, organizations must balance these collaborative efforts with individual workspaces that allow for "deep work," enabling staff to focus on complex analysis and the actionable tasks that translate collaborative ideas into real-world impact.
