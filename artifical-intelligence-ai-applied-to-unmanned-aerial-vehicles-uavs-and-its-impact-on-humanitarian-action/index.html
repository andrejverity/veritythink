Artifical Intelligence (AI) Applied to Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) And its Impact on Humanitarian Action

Written with Camille Oren; May 2020

AI generated summary:

This report explores how the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) is transforming the capabilities of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) within the humanitarian sector. As drone hardware and AI-driven image analysis become more sophisticated, these tools are increasingly used for tasks such as rapid mapping, damage assessment, and monitoring humanitarian situations in hard-to-reach areas. The shift toward more advanced data processing allows humanitarian organizations to quickly translate aerial imagery into actionable insights, significantly improving the speed and quality of disaster response compared to traditional methods.

A significant portion of current humanitarian applications centers on post-flight data analysis, where machine learning and deep learning models are used to classify images, detect objects, and map infrastructure. These AI models, often trained using transfer learning to improve accuracy on limited datasets, enable faster decision-making after disasters. Furthermore, the report examines the emerging potential for in-flight AI, where onboard processing allows drones to perform feature detection in real-time, as well as future possibilities such as autonomous drone swarms that could enhance search and rescue operations.

Despite the potential benefits, the adoption of these technologies presents substantial operational, ethical, and regulatory challenges. The report emphasizes that humanitarian actors must prioritize responsible usage by ensuring data privacy, maintaining transparency, and collaborating with local authorities to avoid hindering existing relief efforts. There is a critical need to build local capacity and ensure that humanitarian drone missions are sustainable and ethically grounded, particularly as the technology moves toward higher degrees of autonomy and complex decision-making.
