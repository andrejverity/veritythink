AI Summary of Optimizing Data for AI Chatbots

According to the report from January 2026, even the most advanced AI models can experience a performance drop of up to 59% when moving from clean data to datasets with real-world imperfections. To mitigate this, data providers are encouraged to follow specific formatting guidelines, such as keeping file sizes under 25MB and ensuring each sheet contains only one table with no merged cells or blank rows. Additionally, "wide" formatting—using more columns and fewer rows—is recommended to increase AI accuracy.

The report identifies two primary ways AI chatbots process data: "Calculator Mode," which uses computer scripts for mathematical precision but may ignore context, and "Reader Mode," which treats data like text for better context but suffers from "memory fatigue". In current performance testing, Google Gemini 3 Pro was rated "High" for its ability to handle large OCHA datasets correctly on the first attempt, while ChatGPT 5 was rated "Low" due to failures on files over 30MB.

To achieve the best results, end-users should provide AI with clear roles and specific goals rather than simply asking for a general analysis. Utilizing "Chain-of-Thought" prompting, where the AI is asked to show its work step-by-step, is a critical strategy for ensuring complex math is processed methodically. Users are also advised to start fresh chat sessions for new complex analyses to avoid the "forgetting" that occurs when the chat history consumes the model's context window
