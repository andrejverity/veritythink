Guidance for Collaborating with Volunteers & Technical Communities

Guidance for Collaborating with Volunteers & Technical Communities

Written with Luis Capelo and Natalie Chang; August 2012

Read the full report

AI generated summary:

In the contemporary humanitarian landscape, the emergence of Volunteer and Technical Communities (V&TCs) has revolutionized how data is collected, processed, and analyzed during crises. These volunteer-based networks apply high-level technical skills—ranging from GIS mapping and data scraping to real-time social media monitoring—to support formal relief operations. By operating with an open-source ideology and flexible, non-hierarchical structures, these communities provide a scalable "cognitive surplus" that allows humanitarian organizations to manage massive influxes of information that would otherwise overwhelm traditional response capacities.

Successful engagement with these technical networks requires a structured approach across four critical stages: pre-deployment, activation, deployment, and post-deployment. Effective collaboration begins with building relationships before a disaster occurs, ensuring that information needs are clearly matched with a community's specific technical expertise. During active operations, maintaining dedicated focal points and providing consistent feedback are essential to sustaining volunteer motivation and ensuring the quality and accuracy of the digital outputs.

Every deployment is unique and should be planned around four key characteristics: information complexity, volunteer crowd size, intensity of engagement, and specific technical capacities. Whether tracking refugee movements or updating baseline disaster preparedness reports, organizations must define clear parameters, agree on data security standards, and establish transparent workflows. This collaborative framework not only enhances situational awareness in the field but also fosters a culture of innovation, pushing the humanitarian sector to adopt cutting-edge tools for more efficient crisis management.
